The Theory of Relativity, developed by Albert Einstein, is actually two interconnected theories: Special Relativity (1905) and General Relativity (1915). Special relativity describes how space and time are not absolute but are linked and relative to an observer's frame of reference, with the speed of light remaining constant for all observers.General relativity then extends this to explain gravity not as a force, but as the warping of space and time by mass
